Rebranding Chicago's historic Shedd Aquarium.

I chose to rebrand Shedd Aquarium for this project since it was and still is a nostalgic and dear place to me as a Chicago native, having visited the place many times as a child and teen. My goal for this rebranding was to emphasize the wonder and excitement I felt from the aquarium as a child while bringing out the highlights and its conservatory work.

Class: Systems for Brands, Spring 2021

Time: 2.17.2021 to 5.14.2021

Project: Choose a company, product line, or cultural instution, etc. to rebrand in its entirety, beginning with research and ending with the Visual Identity Guidelines.

Scope: Semester-long independent branding project.

Responsibilities: background research, visual identity development, including logo, color palette, graphic system, and typography, and appropriate sample applications.

After completing background research and an audience analysis, I created multiple moodboards to refine and finalize the visual identity and aesthetic I felt fit best.

FINAL BRAND IDENTITY
For my final logo iteration, I created the logo by illustrating the silhouette of two corals, staghorn and elkhorn, and intersecting the two as a window to an aquarium tank. The two corals are ones Shedd Aquarium and SECORE International monitored in the Caribbean to develop strategies for restoring coral populations in the wild, and I wanted the logo to reflect the aquarium's conservatory identity.
For sample applications, I had the opportunity to explore the numerous options I could see the aquarium applying as a cultural institution in an urban site. I wanted the imagery to make full use of the vibrancy and wildlife diversity in the institution.

The other graphics are the silhouettes of the staghorn and elkhorn corals, which are applied in later merchandise such as tote bags and enamel pins.
