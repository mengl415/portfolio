Reinventing the digital signage platform for RXD, laboratory, and science functions.

During Johnson & Johnson Design Studio's 10-week professional program, our collaborative intern project was to consider how we might utilize digital signage to create a more connected, communicative, and joyful workplace environment while creating a fluid user-experience for both our target audience and the designers and content strategists on the back-end.

Johnson & Johnson Design Studio Intern Project

Time: 10 weeks

Opportunity: Leverage digital signage to create a more connected and joyful workplace environment while considering hybrid workplace models; create and curate a smooth user-experience and a content creation and management strategy for the back-end; and create a cohesive platform relevant across all US sites.

Scope: for lab, research, and science functions.

Role: UX research (desk research, interviews, surveys, affinity mapping, insight synthesis), UI design, brand design, visual guideline & playbook design.

Team: Sophia Geanacopoulos (insights and experiences), Bryce Yao (industrial design), Navya Salwan (digital design).
For our research phase, we conducted both secondary and primary research. Our secondary research consisted of desk research and analogous case studies of Korbyt, the digital signage platform that would be used, and other similar content management systems.

Our primary research consisted of surveys and user interviews of both employees in R&D/science functions and content managers/strategists for Johnson & Johnson's current digital signage systems. We then affinity mapped all our research in Mural and generated insights based on our research, which then led us to our user archetypes and how-might-we opportunities.
In our designing and prototyping phase, we began with a content strategy, dividing content into buckets and organizing it based on global and local communications. We then designed initial wireframes and a few populated key screens for user testing and feedback, which we conducted in two rounds with improvements between each.
For our final presented solution, we created final templates for content managers to utilize and populated them, as well as created a playbook that provides guidance on the brand guidelines, content strategy and hierarchy, and the basics of navigating through Figma. It also includes information on exporting the signage from Figma into Korbyt and what elements of the templates are placeholders to be replaced with in Korbyt.

We felt that Figma was the best space for the templates and playbook to live in so it could continue to evolve and change with the company's guidelines and endeavors as needed. In addition to the templates, example screens, and playbook themselves, we have also created type styles, color styles, and created components for the J&J variable typeface, textures, icons, and our own shape system addition to the brand.
